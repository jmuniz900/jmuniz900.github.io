About me

Born in Guadalajara, Jalisco and raised in Fullerton, California, I aspired to try and gain a life here in the United States. My parents sacrificed their old life to try and allow their children to gain a new one here. As the eldest of four, I've had a rough time trying to learn what life had to offer. With very little guidance in hand, I graduated from Buena Park High School with my high school diploma and California State University, Fullerton with my Bachelors of Science in Computer Science.

As time passed, I've figured I really like the aspect of coding. I've been looking for ways to improve my understanding of how computers and programs work in both the front-end and back-end to become a full-stack developer. The only goal fueling me to keep learning is having my own room. Being of poverty, I've never had my own room, and working for it is what is in my mind right now.

Since I was born in Mexico, I've also had a hard time not being a citizen. It wasn't until the Development, Relief, and Education for Alien Minors Act, or DREAM Act, of 2017 that gave me more of a chance of being able to work & learn from higher education in the United States.
